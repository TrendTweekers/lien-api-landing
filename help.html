Broker Program & Payouts

How do broker commissions work?

You choose one of two commission models:

  • $500 one-time per referred customer (paid once, after the hold period)
  • $50/month recurring per active referred customer (paid monthly, after the hold period for each payment)

The model is set per broker and applies to future referrals unless changed by admin.

When do I get paid?

Commissions are held for 60 days after the FIRST successful customer payment date. For recurring commissions, each monthly invoice has its own 60-day hold period. After the hold period, eligible commissions move into "Due to Pay" status and can be paid in the next payout batch.

What happens if a customer refunds, disputes, or cancels?

Refunded/chargebacked payments do not become eligible for payout. Failed payments also void unpaid earnings. If a subscription cancels or becomes past-due, recurring commissions stop for that customer until payments resume.

Why is my commission showing "On Hold"?

"On Hold" means the 60-day hold window hasn't passed yet. Once it passes, it becomes "Due to Pay" automatically.

How do payouts get processed?

Admin pays brokers in payout batches. Each batch contains one or more eligible ("Due to Pay") commission events and is marked "Paid" together with a timestamp and optional transaction reference.

What payment methods are supported?

We support PayPal, Wise (TransferWise), Revolut, SEPA Transfer (Europe), SWIFT/Wire Transfer, and Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin/USDT). Set your preferred method in your broker dashboard.

Payout Examples

Example 1: $500 One-Time Bounty

  • Day 0: Customer pays first invoice → Commission status: "On Hold"
  • Day 60: Hold period expires → Commission status: "Due to Pay"
  • Day 65: Admin processes payout → Commission status: "Paid"

Note: Only one $500 payment per customer, regardless of how many invoices they pay.

Example 2: $50/Month Recurring

  • Month 1, Day 0: Customer pays first invoice → $50 commission status: "On Hold"
  • Month 1, Day 60: First hold expires → $50 commission status: "Due to Pay"
  • Month 2, Day 0: Customer pays second invoice → New $50 commission status: "On Hold"
  • Month 2, Day 60: Second hold expires → Second $50 commission status: "Due to Pay"
  • Month 3+: Process repeats for each monthly payment

Note: Each monthly invoice has its own 60-day hold period.

Example 3: Refund/Chargeback Scenario

  • Day 0: Customer pays invoice → Commission status: "On Hold"
  • Day 30: Customer requests refund → Commission status: "Voided" (never becomes "Due to Pay")
  • Day 60: Hold period would have expired, but commission remains voided

Note: Refunded, chargebacked, or failed payments void the associated commission earnings.
