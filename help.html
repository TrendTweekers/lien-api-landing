Frequently Asked Questions

What is a mechanics lien?

A mechanics lien (also called a construction lien or materialman's lien) is a legal claim against a property for unpaid work or materials provided for construction or improvement. Filing a mechanics lien gives you a security interest in the property, which can help ensure payment.

How accurate are your calculations?

Our calculations are based on current state statutes and are updated quarterly. We cover all 50 US states + DC with 100% accuracy based on published laws.

Important: Our calculator is a tool, not legal advice. Always verify critical deadlines with a licensed construction attorney, especially for high-value claims.

Which states do you cover?

We cover all 50 US states plus the District of Columbia (51 jurisdictions total). This includes complex states like Texas (month+day formulas), California (Notice of Completion triggers), and Hawaii (shortest 45-day deadline in the US).

How do I use the API?

Our API allows you to integrate lien deadline calculations into your own applications. To get started:

  1. Request an API key from our API page
  2. Review the API documentation
  3. Make POST requests to /api/v1/calculate-deadline
  4. Include your API key in the Authorization header

What happens if I miss a deadline?

Missing a mechanics lien deadline typically means you lose your right to file a lien for that project. This can result in forfeiting tens of thousands of dollars in unpaid receivables. That's why we built LienDeadline—to help you never miss a deadline.
