Arizona is High Risk for Suppliers

Arizona's 20-day preliminary notice is unforgiving. If notice is late, lien rights are reduced or lost entirely — even if the owner knew you were supplying materials.

Arizona Lien Deadlines

Deadline TypeTimingDetails
20-Day Preliminary Notice Within 20 days of first furnishing Required for material suppliers. Late notice limits lien rights to materials supplied in the prior 20 days.
Lien Filing Deadline 120 days from project completion Measured from completion of the project as a whole, not last delivery.
Notice Method Certified / registered mail Mailing method matters. Proof of service is critical.
Enforcement Deadline 6 months from filing Lien must be enforced through legal action within six months.

Common Supplier Mistakes in Arizona

  • Sending the 20-day notice late and assuming it "still counts."
  • Using invoice date instead of first delivery date.
  • Assuming owner awareness replaces notice requirements.
  • Missing the 6-month enforcement deadline after filing.
