⚠️ Critical NC Requirement: Notice to Lien Agent

For projects over $30,000 involving a residential or commercial improvement (where a Lien Agent is designated), you MUST serve a Notice to Lien Agent. This should be done within 15 days of first furnishing or before the property is sold/mortgaged, otherwise your lien rights may be subordinated or lost.

Key Deadlines for North Carolina Suppliers

Deadline Type Timing Details
Notice to Lien Agent ASAP (Best within 15 days of first work) Required on projects >$30k with a designated Lien Agent. Use the LiensNC system or serve physically to protect priority.
File Claim of Lien Within 120 days of last furnishing Must be filed with the Clerk of Superior Court in the county where the property is located.
Serve Claim of Lien Simultaneous with filing You must serve a copy of the Claim of Lien on the owner. Best practice is to do this at the same time as filing.
Enforce Lien (Lawsuit) Within 180 days of last furnishing The lawsuit to enforce the lien must be filed within 180 days of your last furnishing date (not the filing date!).
Statutes NC General Statutes Chapter 44A, Article 2; § 44A-7 et seq.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Ignoring the Lien Agent

Failing to notify the designated Lien Agent is a major pitfall in NC. If the property is sold or refinanced before you give notice, your lien rights can be wiped out.

Missing the 120-Day Filing

The deadline is strictly 120 days from the last date of furnishing labor or materials. Filing on day 121 makes the lien invalid.

Confusing Lawsuit Deadline

In NC, you must file suit within 180 days of last furnishing, not 180 days from the lien filing. This is a shorter window than many other states.

Improper Service

The Claim of Lien must be served on the owner. Failure to prove service can be a defense against your lien claim.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the "LiensNC" system?

LiensNC is an online system used in North Carolina to identify Lien Agents and file Notices to Lien Agent. It is the central hub for preliminary notices on larger projects.

Can I lien public projects in NC?

No. You cannot file a mechanics lien against public property (state or local government). You must instead make a claim against the payment bond (Little Miller Act).

Does the Notice to Lien Agent expire?

Generally, once properly filed/served for a project, it covers your furnishing. However, you should ensure it accurately reflects your company details.

What constitutes "last furnishing"?

It is the last date you provided contractually required labor or materials. Punch list work or warranty work usually does NOT extend this deadline.

Can I include interest?

Yes, if your contract provides for it. Otherwise, you may be entitled to the legal rate of interest from the date of breach, but it must be pleaded correctly.
