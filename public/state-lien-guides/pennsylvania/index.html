Pennsylvania is High Risk for Suppliers

Pennsylvania has a strict "Notice of Intent" requirement. You must serve this notice at least 30 days before you file your actual lien claim. If you wait until the last minute of your filing deadline (6 months), you will be too late to send the notice, forfeiting your rights.

Pennsylvania Lien Deadlines

Deadline Type Timing Details
Notice of Intent to Lien 30 days before filing Must be formally served on the owner at least 30 days before you file the Mechanics' Lien Claim.
Lien Filing (Mechanics' Lien) 6 months from last furnishing Must file the lien claim with the Prothonotary within 6 months of your completion of work or last furnishing of materials.
Service of Lien Within 1 month of filing After filing, you must serve the owner with the lien claim within 1 month. An affidavit of service must be filed within 20 days of service.
Enforcement Period 2 years from filing You must file a Complaint to Enforce Judgment on the lien within 2 years of filing the lien claim.
Statutes PA Mechanics' Lien Law 49 P.S. § 1101 et seq.; § 1501; § 1502; § 1701

Common Supplier Mistakes in Pennsylvania

Waiting Until Month 6 to Start

If you wait until the 6th month to think about your lien, it's too late. You need to send the Notice of Intent 30 days BEFORE filing. Start by month 4 or 5 at the latest.

Failing to Serve the Filed Lien

Filing the lien is not enough. You must serve the owner with the filed claim within 1 month and file proof of service. Missing this step invalidates the lien.

Waiving Lien Rights

Be careful with contracts. Pennsylvania allows subcontractors to waive lien rights in certain situations (though restricted for residential in some cases). Read your contract carefully.

Not Using Proper Service

Service of the Notice of Intent and the Lien Claim must be done formally (usually by sheriff or certified mail depending on the document). Casual email or regular mail is often insufficient.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Notice of Intent due?

You must serve the Notice of Intent at least 30 days before you file your lien. Since the lien must be filed within 6 months, the absolute latest you should serve the Notice of Intent is 5 months after last furnishing.

What is the filing deadline?

The Mechanics' Lien Claim must be filed with the Prothonotary in the county where the property is located within 6 months of your last date of furnishing labor or materials.

Can I waive my lien rights in PA?

Yes, in some cases. Pennsylvania law allows subcontractors to waive lien rights if they sign a waiver, or if the general contractor has filed a "Stipulation Against Liens" before work began. Always check the public records and your contract.

Do I need a preliminary notice at the start?

Generally, no. Pennsylvania doesn't have a "Notice to Owner" required at the start of the project like Florida or California. The first required notice is the Notice of Intent to Lien (30 days before filing).

Does the deadline include punch list work?

Typically, no. Punch list work, warranty work, or minor repairs usually do not extend the lien deadline. Calculate your 6 months from the date of substantial completion of your contract work.
