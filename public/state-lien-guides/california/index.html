Why California is high-risk for suppliers

California punishes delays. If your preliminary notice window or lien/enforcement deadlines are missed, you can lose leverage fast — even when the delivery tickets are perfect.
  • 20-day notice window starts when you first furnish materials (not when you invoice).
  • Project-wide completion controls the lien filing window (90 days after completion of the overall project).
  • Enforcement is also short: 90 days after recording.
  • Mailing method matters: use the allowed mailing options to create proof of delivery.

If you ship on multiple POs or phases, treat notice + deadlines as a system — not a one-time task.

Common California mistakes suppliers make

  • Waiting for the first past-due invoice before sending preliminary notice (the clock starts at first furnishing).
  • Assuming "90 days from last delivery" — California's filing deadline here is tied to project completion as a whole.
  • Mailing without proof (use registered, certified, or first-class with certificate of mailing).
  • Recording the lien but not calendaring enforcement (enforcement is due 90 days after recording).
  • Not standardizing notice on every job — inconsistent notice is how good AR gets trapped.

California supplier FAQs

Who should send a California preliminary notice?
Based on the supplier-focused rule set here, material suppliers typically send preliminary notice. If you're not in direct contract with the owner, treat notice as the default safety step.
When does the 20-day preliminary notice clock start?
The timing rule provided is within 20 days of first providing materials. For suppliers, "first furnishing" is the operational trigger — not invoice date.
What's the lien filing deadline for California suppliers?
Record the claim within 90 days after completion of the project as a whole (per the provided supplier rule).
How long do I have to enforce a recorded lien in California?
The enforcement window provided is 90 days after recording the claim. Calendar this the same day you record.
How should a California preliminary notice be sent?
The notice method listed is registered mail, certified mail, or first-class mail with a certificate of mailing. Choose a method that preserves proof for your file.
