⚠️ Critical Ohio Requirement: Notice of Furnishing

In Ohio, if a Notice of Commencement is filed, you MUST serve a Notice of Furnishing on the owner and original contractor within 21 days of first furnishing labor or materials. Failure to do so generally limits your lien rights to the 21 days prior to when the notice is finally served.

Key Deadlines for Ohio Suppliers

Deadline Type Timing Details
Notice of Furnishing Within 21 days of first furnishing Required if a Notice of Commencement is filed. Send to owner and general contractor to preserve full lien rights.
File Lien Affidavit 75 days (Commercial) / 60 days (Residential) Must be filed with the County Recorder within 75 days (commercial/industrial) or 60 days (residential) of your last furnishing date.
Serve Copy of Lien Within 30 days of filing You must serve a copy of the recorded affidavit on the owner (or lessee/designee) within 30 days of filing.
Enforce Lien (Lawsuit) Within 6 years Suit to foreclose must be brought within 6 years of filing, unless the owner serves a Notice to Commence Suit (reducing it to 60 days).
Statutes Ohio Revised Code ORC § 1311.01 et seq.; § 1311.05; § 1311.06; § 1311.07

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Missing the 21-Day Window

Waiting too long to send the Notice of Furnishing is the #1 error. If you miss the 21-day window, you lose lien rights for everything furnished before 21 days prior to the late notice.

Wrong Filing Deadline

Ohio has different deadlines for commercial (75 days) vs. residential (60 days) projects. Don't assume you have 75 days on a residential job.

Failure to Serve Copy

Recording the lien isn't enough. You must serve a copy on the owner within 30 days and file an affidavit of service, or the lien can be voided.

Ignoring Notice to Commence Suit

If an owner serves a "Notice to Commence Suit," you must file your foreclosure lawsuit within 60 days, or your lien is void.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a Notice of Furnishing always required in Ohio?

It is required if the owner (or their agent) has filed a "Notice of Commencement" for the project. Since you might not know if one is filed, it is best practice to always send a Notice of Furnishing.

What if I supply to a subcontractor?

Suppliers to subcontractors have lien rights in Ohio, provided they comply with the Notice of Furnishing and filing deadlines. Suppliers to suppliers generally do not.

Does the 75-day deadline start from invoice date?

No. It starts from the last date you physically furnished labor or materials to the project site. Invoice dates are irrelevant for the statutory deadline.

Can I include attorney fees in my lien amount?

Generally, no. In Ohio, you typically cannot include attorney fees or collection costs in the lien face value, only the contract balance plus interest if agreed.

How do I extend the lien?

Ohio mechanics liens are valid for 6 years unless forced earlier. There is no simple "extension" document; you must file a foreclosure suit to enforce it.
